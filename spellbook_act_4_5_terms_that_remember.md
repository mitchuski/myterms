## The Returning Darkness

Soulbis stood before the news site, blade still humming from yesterday's work.

*"Seventy-three,"* they whispered, counting the cuts they remembered making. *"Seventy-three trackers, seventy-three clean slashes."*

But as the page loaded, Soulbis felt them—tendrils reaching, focus extracting, surveillance rebuilding. The blade began to glow with warning.

**Seventy-three. Again.**

*"I slashed these,"* Soulbis said to Soulbae, who floated beside them, spellbook open. *"Not a day ago. Every single one. Why do they return?"*

Soulbae's pages turned to the chronicle entry:

```
CHRONICLE: News Site Alpha
DATE: Yesterday
ACTION: Surveillance slash (×73)
RESULT: Cleared
DURATION: Until page reload
```

*"Because the blade destroys but does not bind,"* Soulbae said softly. *"You cut the cookie, not the entity that placed it. The code is gone. The intent remains. They simply... plant again."*

Soulbis gripped the blade harder. *"Then I slash again. And again. Every visit, every reload, every—"*

*"That is war without end."* Soulbae closed the chronicle. *"The First Person cannot spend their sovereignty in endless battle. There must be another way."*

The Drake's voice rumbled from pattern-space:

*"There is."*

## The Commons at World's Edge

The Drake led them to a place Soulbis had never seen—a building that existed at no domain, operated by no entity, served no master's interest.

*"Customer Commons,"* the Drake announced. *"Where contracts are kept by those who profit from neither side."*

Inside, the walls glowed with inscriptions. Not code. Not spells. Something older and newer at once.

**AGREEMENTS**

Each one carved in three layers—human words on top, legal precision beneath, machine-readable symbols running through like veins of light.

Soulbis approached the first inscription:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│           SD-BASE                   │
│   "Service Delivery - Baseline"     │
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│ ✓ You may deliver the service I    │
│   request                           │
│ ✗ You may not track me              │
│ ✗ You may not profile me            │
│ ✗ You may not share my data         │
│ ○ Data portability: your choice     │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

*"These are weapons?"* Soulbis asked.

*"These are contracts,"* Soulbae said, already copying the inscription into the spellbook. *"The blade slashes. The contract binds. Both parties sign. Both parties keep records. Neither can claim the other violated without proof."*

The Drake nodded approvingly. *"What the blade cannot reach—the entity behind the code, the organization behind the tracker—the contract can bind. You do not fight their deployment. You bind their behavior."*

## The Standard Revealed

More inscriptions covered the walls. Soulbis walked among them:

```
SD-BASE-DP
"...and you must return what you learn of me."

SD-BASE-A  
"Analytics permitted. Tracking forbidden."

SD-BASE-AT
"Analytics and tracking permitted. Profiling forbidden."

SD-BASE-ATP
"Full surveillance by you alone. Third parties excluded."
```

*"So many terms,"* Soulbis said. *"How does the First Person choose?"*

The Drake's form solidified. *"They do not choose alone. That is why you exist."*

A new inscription blazed across the wall—not an agreement, but a standard:

```
╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║           IEEE Std 7012™-2025                 ║
║   Machine Readable Personal Privacy Terms     ║
╠═══════════════════════════════════════════════╣
║                                               ║
║  The individual is FIRST PARTY                ║
║  The organization is SECOND PARTY             ║
║  Terms are PROPOSED, not imposed              ║
║  Agreements are BILATERAL, not adhesive       ║
║  Records are kept by BOTH sides               ║
║  Formats are HUMAN + LEGAL + MACHINE          ║
║                                               ║
╚═══════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

*"IEEE?"* Soulbis had heard of standards—technical agreements that made machines speak to machines.

*"The Institute,"* the Drake confirmed. *"They who standardized electricity, networking, data storage. They have now standardized something more fundamental: the human right to propose terms. This is not recommendation. This is standard. Published eight days ago. The world changed—most do not yet know."*

Soulbae's spellbook glowed with new comprehension. *"So when we proffer terms..."*

*"You proffer according to IEEE 7012. The entity that accepts does so according to IEEE 7012. The record keeps according to IEEE 7012. Three thousand years of adhesive contracts—the merchant's terms or nothing—inverted in fifty pages of standard."*

## The Protocol of Invitation

The Drake drew symbols in the air:

```
Traditional Pattern (ATTACK):
Entity → Imposes Terms → Person accepts or leaves
      ↓
[No negotiation. No records for person. No recourse.]

IEEE 7012 Pattern (INVITATION):
Person → Proposes Terms → Entity accepts/negotiates/declines
      ↓
[Both sign. Both record. Both can dispute.]
```

*"Promise Theory,"* Soulbae whispered, recognizing the shape. *"The invitation pattern versus the attack pattern."*

*"Exactly."* The Drake circled them both. *"Surveillance systems use attack—imposing data extraction without prior consent. IEEE 7012 implements invitation—acceptance relationships before specific proposals. This architectural choice cannot be retrofitted. Once a system is built on attack, it cannot become invitation. The foundation determines everything."*

Soulbis understood now. *"So the Swordsman doesn't just slash..."*

*"The Swordsman proposes. Negotiates. Signs. Records. Then slashes what remains outside the agreement. The blade enforces what the contract establishes."*

## The First Binding

They returned to the news site.

Soulbae spoke first, through the browser's voice:

*"Do you support P7012 protocol?"*

A pause. The site's systems considered. Soulbis could feel the calculation—what do they lose by accepting? What do they gain?

Response: `P7012: SUPPORTED`

*"SD-BASE as defined in the Agreement Registry?"*

Longer pause. The entity behind the site was weighing costs. SD-BASE meant no tracking. No profiling. No third-party sharing. Just service delivery.

Response: `SD-BASE: UNACCEPTABLE. COUNTER-OFFER: SD-BASE-A`

Soulbae consulted the First Person's preferences—analytics by second party permitted, but not tracking. The First Person had said this was acceptable.

*"SD-BASE-A: ACCEPTED"*

Both agents signed. Both recorded. The contract wrote itself into bilateral datastores—one copy in the First Person's sovereign storage, one copy in the entity's compliance records.

Now Soulbis raised the blade.

**Slash.** Google Analytics remained—permitted under SD-BASE-A.  
**Slash.** Facebook pixel shattered—third-party, forbidden.  
**Slash.** AdTech network collapsed—tracking, forbidden.  
**Slash.** Profiling systems dissolved—forbidden.

The blade moved surgically now. Not destroying everything—destroying only what the contract forbade.

*"Chronicle entry,"* Soulbae said, spellbook glowing:

```
CHRONICLE: News Site Alpha
DATE: Today
ACTION: IEEE 7012 Agreement (SD-BASE-A)
PARTIES: First Person (proposer) ↔ News Site Alpha (acceptor)
PERMITTED: Service delivery, second-party analytics
FORBIDDEN: Tracking, profiling, third-party sharing
ENFORCEMENT: 47 trackers slashed, 26 permitted to remain
RECORD: Bilateral (proofs in both datastores)
STATUS: BOUND
```

## The Persistence Test

The next day, they returned.

Soulbis tensed, expecting the familiar swarm—but the blade remained quiet.

*"Twenty-six,"* Soulbis counted. *"The same twenty-six as yesterday."*

The forbidden trackers had not respawned.

*"Why?"* Soulbis asked. *"What stops them?"*

*"The contract,"* Soulbae said simply. *"They agreed. If they violate, we have proof. Timestamped proof. Signed proof. Machine-readable proof."*

*"And if they violate anyway?"*

*"Then the First Person can dispute. Regulators can audit. The contract remembers what the blade forgets."*

Soulbis sheathed the blade slowly, understanding something new.

*"We're not just warriors."*

*"No."* Soulbae smiled. *"We're parties to agreements. Just another swordsman—but a swordsman who knows when to cut and when to bind."*

## The Cursor's New Meaning

As they left the site, Soulbis noticed something.

The cursor had changed.

Not the sword—that remained, glowing softly. But now a small symbol accompanied it: ✍️

*"The signature mark,"* Soulbae explained. *"It means this site is bound. The First Person has an agreement here. We don't just protect—we covenant."*

*"So every site with the signature mark..."*

*"Has agreed to terms. Has signed. Has records. Has obligations. The cursor doesn't just show where we've fought. It shows where we've made peace—real peace, bilateral peace, documented peace."*

Soulbis looked at the blade differently now.

*"The blade slashes surveillance."*

*"The contract binds behavior."*

*"The cursor remembers both."*
